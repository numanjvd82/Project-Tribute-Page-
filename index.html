Abdul Sattar Edhi

The Man who gave his everything to humanity

14 things to know about Abdul Sattar Edhi

  • Abdul Sattar Edhi began his humanitarian work in Pakistan soon after partition in 1947
  • He set up the Edhi Foundation with just 500 $.
  • He began by working for the people of Karachi and went on to build centers offering various services.
  • To reduce female infanticide a cradle was place outside each centre.
  • He established orphanages which saved over 50,000 babies.
  • Most of these children went on to live with the families that cherished them.
  • By Pakistani law, a Father's name is needed for official Paperwork.
    Edhi gave his name to the fatherless.
    Over the years, he 'fathered' hundreds of thousands of People.
  • Edhi also won a case for Orphans to receive national identity cards.
  • Today, There are over 300 welfare centres across the country.
  • 24-hour medical dispensaries,
    A hotline for missing people.
    Shelters for women and the elderly.
  • The largest morgue in Pakistan and a fleet of over 1,800 ambulances, 28 rescue boats and 2 airplanes.
  • Abdul Sattar Edhi died on 8 July 2016
  • He was given a State Funeral.
